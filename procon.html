.Tiff
Pros Cons
Can be used with most computer software and applications Difficult or impossible to email, upload or present because of size
16 bit capability Can not be used with most online photo sharing sites
Helps prevent posterization, banding and compression artifacts Images take up a lot of space on your hard drive (most scanned by SSP are approximately 110MB each)
Uncompressed image is not "lossy", but maintains high quality detail
Standard for most printing companies
Better for personal editing
Best quality available
.Jpg
Pros Cons
Easy to email, upload or present because of size only 8 bit capability, not 16
Can be used with most computer software and applications Vulnerable to posterization, banding and compression artifacts
Can be used with most online photo sharing sites "Lossy" image, not as much detail as a TIFF image
Smaller images are easier to store on your hard-drive (approximately 5-7 MB each) Can be used in most printing companies, but may be subjected to smaller sized prints
High quality - but not the best Personal editing is possible but not recommended for many images
